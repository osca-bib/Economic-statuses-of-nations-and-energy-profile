    Efficiency and Regional Trends

    Exploring regional trends of energy efficiency.

    Least Efficient Economies (Energy consumed per $ GDP)

    This is a bar graph of the 15 least energy-efficient economies based on their energy intensity (Total energy consumption divided by GDP) in order. This chart reveals that low energy efficiency is not only tied to a low GDP, as shown by nations with larger GDPs on the graph. Further, it shows that countries with similar GDPs can have different energy intensities, implying that factors other than economic output influence energy usage. The graph also reveals regional trends in energy inefficiency around former Soviet, Central Asian, and Middle Eastern nations.

    Global Energy Intensity

    This is a choropleth map of global energy intensity. The graph reveals that Western Europe has one of the most efficient regional energy intensities. On the other hand, it also shows that Eastern Europe and the Middle East have one of the least efficient regional energy intensities, implying that they may be reliant on energy-intensive industries. Overall, it also reveals that regional neighbors have similar levels of energy intensity, meaning that nearby countries can influence energy policy changes.

    Energy Consumption vs Renewable Share

    This figure is a scatter plot of the percent of renewable sources within a nation against total energy used per capita, using population as the size of the countries. This graph reveals that higher percentage of renewable energy on average, is linked to a slight decrease in energy consumed per capita. It additionally shows that most of the world still does not primarily use renewable energy as its main energy source. It also reveals that countries with high populations do not use large amounts of renewable energies, most using under 25%.
