    Energy Change Over Time

    Exploring energy change and trends over time.

    Global Growth Comparison for Renewable Energy

    This is a line graph that analyzes the growth of 3 renewable energy sources in TWh over the past 20 years. It reveals that older renewables, specifically hydro, have maintained small growth over the past 20 years. On the other hand, modern renewable energies have grown exponentially in comparison to the older ones. Overall, it shows that the transition to renewable energy is not based on old technologies but newer evolving technologies.

    Coal Consumption of Great Powers (2000-2020)

    This is a line graph that analyzes the coal consumption of the same powerful nations as in the renewable share change graph, instead of graphing their coal consumption from 2000-2020. This graph demonstrates that most powerful nations, except China, have either minimally increased their coal usage or decreased it. It also indicates that usage of unclean energies is not decreasing at a quick rate, posing a problem for the shift to clean energy. Further, it shows that the world is still reliant on coal as a fuel source, meaning that there are low odds of rapidly transitioning away from it unless a large technological breakthrough occurs.

    Renewable Share Change (2000 vs. 2020)

    This graph is a bar chart plotting five economically powerful and influential countries' growth in renewable energy percentage from 2000 to 2020. This reveals that some major nations, such as China, the United States, and the United Kingdom, have shifted rapidly to renewable energy over the past 20 years, though they still do not primarily rely on it. It also demonstrates that other larger nations, such as Russia and India, have not increased their renewable energy share much over the past 20 years. This overall reveals that economic power does not lead to a higher percentage of renewable energy usage, meaning that there could be other underlying factors, such as internal politics and international alliances.

    Total Energy Consumption by Type (2000 vs. 2020)

    This is a pie chart of the global energy mix in 2000 and 2020. The graph shows that renewable energy has grown significantly since 2000. It also shows that despite the growth of renewable energy sources, non-renewable energy sources have grown at a higher percentage, indicating that the world is not currently succeeding in becoming carbon neutral. Further, it shows that despite some non-renewable energy sources going up as a whole, carbon usage specifically has gone down.
